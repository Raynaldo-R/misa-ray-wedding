Getting Here

ferries, drives & questions

Ferry schedules ↗ · Wait times ↗ · Google Maps ↗

Where is Port Gamble?

Port Gamble is on the Kitsap Peninsula, north of Kingston on WA-104. ZIP 98370.

Open in Google Maps ↗

Which ferry do I take from Seattle?

Port Gamble has no ferry dock. You cross the Sound and drive a short way in. Two good options:

Edmonds → Kingston: about a 25-minute crossing, then roughly 10 minutes on WA-104 into Port Gamble. Best if you're coming from north Seattle or up I-5.
Edmonds–Kingston schedule ↗

Seattle → Bainbridge Island: about a 35-minute crossing, then a 35-minute drive via SR-305. Best if you're staying in downtown Seattle.
Seattle–Bainbridge schedule ↗

Ferries fill up on summer weekends, so give yourself a buffer. Check current schedules ↗ and live wait times ↗ closer to the date.

Driving up from Oregon or the south?

You can skip the ferry entirely. Take I-5 North to Tacoma, then WA-16 West across the Tacoma Narrows Bridge, then WA-3 North through Bremerton, Silverdale, and Poulsbo into Port Gamble.

The Narrows Bridge toll is only charged heading east (toward Tacoma, on your way home), so the drive to the wedding is free. Narrows toll info ↗

Do I need a car on the ferry?

Probably. There is no transit out to the venues. Walking on is cheaper if you can carpool from the other side. Let us know on your RSVP and we'll help connect carpools.

WSDOT walk-on passenger info ↗

Flying in?

Fly into Sea-Tac (SEA) ↗. From there, head up I-5 to the Edmonds ferry, or into downtown Seattle for the Bainbridge boat. A rental car is the easiest way to get around once you're on the peninsula.
